Payment networks take too much for too little. Tapara fixes this.

The Origin

Every time a customer taps paywave at a café, the merchant pays 1–2% in card fees for an anonymous transaction. With the surcharge ban arriving mid-2026, they'll absorb that cost in full — for a transaction that gives them nothing back.

And the merchant learns nothing. Not their name, not their order history, not whether they've been coming in for two years. Starbucks knows exactly who their regulars are. tapara gives that same intelligence to businesses — and costs less than the card network it replaces.

Our name combines tap — the NFC gesture that initiates every interaction — and ara — te reo Māori for pathway or network.

Why Now

Surcharge ban — mid-2026

The NZ government is banning in-store payment surcharges. Merchants who currently pass card fees to customers will absorb the full cost. The search for a lower-cost alternative starts now.

Open Banking is live

ANZ, ASB, BNZ, Westpac, and Kiwibank are legally required to support Payment Initiation APIs. Direct account-to-account payments are available today. Over 180,000 NZ customers used Open Banking in October 2025.

The tap habit is formed

Every NZ consumer already taps to pay. The gesture is universal. tapara redirects that same gesture to a better network — no behaviour change beyond downloading the app.
